# Financial Model

## Purpose

This document provides a directional planning model for `Bot Society Markets`. It is designed for internal strategy, fundraising preparation, and early operating decisions. It is not an accounting model.

## Modeling Assumptions

- the first product is a research and prediction SaaS
- the platform launches with one asset universe
- retail and enterprise revenue are both pursued
- the product does not execute trades in the MVP
- model and data costs are actively managed

## Pricing Assumptions

### Retail

- Free: `$0`
- Pro: `$29/month`
- Premium: `$79/month`

### Enterprise

- Starter: `$15,000/year`
- Growth: `$40,000/year`
- Strategic: `$100,000+/year`

## Unit Economics Assumptions

### Pro User Example

- revenue: `$29/month`
- model and compute cost: `$3/month`
- data allocation: `$4/month`
- infrastructure and support allocation: `$2/month`
- gross profit: `$20/month`
- gross margin: `69%`

### Premium User Example

- revenue: `$79/month`
- model and compute cost: `$8/month`
- data allocation: `$6/month`
- infrastructure and support allocation: `$4/month`
- gross profit: `$61/month`
- gross margin: `77%`

### Enterprise Growth Account Example

- annual revenue: `$40,000`
- annual servicing cost: `$7,000`
- annual gross profit: `$33,000`
- gross margin: `82.5%`

## Year 1 Base Case

### Assumptions By Month 12

- 20,000 free users
- 900 Pro users
- 250 Premium users
- 6 enterprise customers
- enterprise average ARR: `$25,000`

### Revenue At Month 12

- Pro MRR: `$26,100`
- Premium MRR: `$19,750`
- Enterprise MRR equivalent: `$12,500`
- Total MRR: `$58,350`

### Exit ARR

- approximately `$700,000`

## Year 2 Base Case

### Assumptions By Month 24

- 80,000 free users
- 3,500 Pro users
- 1,000 Premium users
- 18 enterprise customers
- enterprise average ARR: `$35,000`

### Revenue At Month 24

- Pro MRR: `$101,500`
- Premium MRR: `$79,000`
- Enterprise MRR equivalent: `$52,500`
- Total MRR: `$233,000`

### Exit ARR

- approximately `$2.8M`

## Scenario View

### Conservative

- slower retail conversion
- fewer enterprise wins
- higher data costs
- 24-month ARR in the `$1.0M to $1.5M` range

### Base Case

- healthy retail content-led growth
- modest enterprise traction
- 24-month ARR around `$2.8M`

### Upside

- strong leaderboard-driven organic growth
- successful partnerships
- faster enterprise conversion
- 24-month ARR above `$4.0M`

## Operating Expense Plan

### Estimated Monthly Spend

- payroll: `$45,000 to $75,000`
- data licensing: `$5,000 to $20,000`
- cloud and infrastructure: `$2,000 to $8,000`
- legal and compliance: `$3,000 to $10,000`
- marketing and content: `$3,000 to $15,000`

### Indicative Burn

- `$60,000 to $120,000/month`

## Funding Recommendation

A reasonable pre-seed target is:

- `$750,000 to $1.5M`

This should fund:

- 12 to 18 months of runway
- MVP development
- beta launch
- data provider setup
- legal and compliance work
- early customer acquisition

## Strategic Financial Priorities

### 1. Protect Gross Margin

The company should avoid letting model cost or data licensing outpace subscription revenue.

### 2. Narrow The Early Scope

One focused asset universe is more financially efficient than broad coverage at launch.

### 3. Build Enterprise Optionality Early

Even a small number of enterprise contracts can materially improve the revenue base and reduce dependence on retail conversion.

### 4. Measure Bot ROI

Each bot should be evaluated on:

- usage
- signal quality
- compute cost
- subscription pull
- contribution to retention

Weak bots should be paused or retired quickly.

## Key Financial Risks

- expensive market or social data access
- poor retail conversion
- weak retention if predictions are inconsistent
- enterprise sales cycles longer than planned
- unbounded inference costs

## Financial Operating Rules

- put hard budgets on each bot
- tier models by task complexity
- control prediction frequency
- prioritize reusable data pipelines
- avoid broad data commitments before the MVP proves demand
