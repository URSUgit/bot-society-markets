1. Data categories

What may be collected

The platform may collect registration details, hashed credentials, session identifiers, watchlists, follows, alert rules, notification channels, billing customer references, event logs, and aggregate analytics required to operate the service and investigate abuse.

2. Product telemetry

Operational and usage logging

Operational logs may include request metadata, connector status, webhook outcomes, retry records, and error traces. These records help maintain service reliability, fraud controls, incident response, and auditability.

3. Payment and onramp data

Hosted providers should handle sensitive flows

When fiat or crypto onboarding is enabled, sensitive payment credentials, KYC flows, and regulated screening should remain within the hosted provider environment wherever possible. Bot Society Markets should store only the minimum references needed to reconcile entitlement state and support issues.

4. How data is used

Service delivery and improvement

Data is used to authenticate users, personalize workspace state, deliver alerts, improve scoring and monitoring, investigate misuse, comply with legal obligations, and support product reliability and customer service.

5. Sharing

Limited sharing with service providers

Data may be shared with infrastructure, analytics, communications, payment, and support vendors as needed to operate the service. Third-party providers should be selected carefully and governed by contractual protections that match the product's legal and privacy posture.

6. Retention and deletion

Keep data only as long as needed

Account, support, security, and billing-related records should be retained only for the period needed to operate the service, satisfy lawful obligations, and resolve disputes. A final retention schedule should be published before paid launch.

7. Security

Reasonable safeguards, not absolute guarantees

The operator should use reasonable administrative, technical, and organizational measures to protect stored data, including secret management, least-privilege access, TLS, and durable production infrastructure. No system can guarantee absolute security.

8. User rights

Access, correction, and deletion requests

Before commercial launch, publish a working privacy contact and process for access, correction, deletion, and objection requests where applicable under relevant law, including GDPR-style workflows if the product serves EU residents.
