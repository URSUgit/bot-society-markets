Any move toward execution-adjacent features should go through counsel review and explicit user disclosures first.

1. Market risk

Prices can move fast and irrationally

Crypto assets, prediction markets, and related instruments can be extremely volatile, illiquid, gap-prone, and sensitive to news, market structure, and venue outages. Users can lose some or all capital when acting on market ideas.

2. Model risk

Scores and signals can be wrong

Analyst-bot outputs, sentiment readings, smart-money profiles, macro overlays, and simulations depend on assumptions, sampled data, and heuristic logic. They can fail under regime shifts, sparse data, manipulated signals, or implementation mistakes.

3. Backtest and paper-trading risk

Simulation quality is not real execution quality

Historical replays and paper portfolios cannot fully model latency, execution quality, slippage, fees, routing, liquidity, liquidation risk, or user psychology. Strong backtests do not guarantee live profitability.

4. Venue and counterparty risk

External platforms introduce their own exposure

Third-party exchanges, onramps, payment providers, market-data vendors, and distribution channels can fail operationally, restrict access, change terms, impose fees, or become unavailable by jurisdiction.

5. Legal and tax risk

Users remain responsible for their own compliance

Use of market intelligence, digital assets, and event-market products may trigger local regulatory, tax, reporting, or eligibility obligations. Users should obtain their own legal, tax, and financial advice where needed.

6. No personal recommendation

Information is general, not individualized advice

The platform does not assess a user's personal financial circumstances, investment objectives, or risk tolerance. Nothing on the service should be treated as a recommendation tailored to an individual's situation.

7. Operational outages

Downtime and stale data can happen

Maintenance, outages, provider failures, data delays, bad connectors, or misconfigured webhooks can degrade the product. Users should not rely on uninterrupted availability when making time-sensitive decisions.
